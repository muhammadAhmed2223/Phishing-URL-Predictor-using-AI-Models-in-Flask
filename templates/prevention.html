Caution against Phishing Attacks:

  • Verify the sender's email address, especially if the email contains unexpected links or requests.
  • Look for spelling and grammar mistakes, as legitimate organizations usually maintain professional communication.

  • Hover over links before clicking to see the actual URL. Ensure it matches the expected website.
  • Don't click on links in unsolicited emails or messages, even if they seem urgent.

  • Before entering sensitive information, check that the website's URL begins with "https://" and has a padlock icon in the address bar.
  • Avoid entering sensitive data on websites with misspelled URLs or domains that look suspicious.

Enable 2FA whenever possible. This adds an extra layer of security, even if your password is compromised.
